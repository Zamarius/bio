J.Cole

Jermaine Lamar Cole was born January 28th ,1985 in Franfurt,Germany while his dad was based in the US Army. He moved to Fayetteville, North Carolina when he was 8 months old. He started rapping at the age of 12 and went by the name , "Blaza", later swithng to "Therapist". Before releasing "The Come Up" he started going by his present stage name, J. Cole. Jermaine wa an amazing violinist during high school, but went on ot get a degree in business and communications.

He attended and graduated under an academic scholarship from St. John's University in 2007 with a 3.8 GPA. Jermaine didn't recieve his diploma until April 9, 2015 because of a book fine. In 2009, J. Cole attempted to give Jay-Z a copy of his mixtape and stood outside of his house for hour. Although he was turned down that night, that same year J. Cole became the first artist to be signed to Jay-Z's record label, Roc Nation.

Truly Yours was released in anticipation of J. Cole’s 2013 album Born Sinner and contains tracks that didn’t make the cut. It would later be followed by Truly Yours 2.

Friday Night Lights serves as J. Cole’s third official mixtape and was released to help hold over fans until his debut album, Cole World: The Sideline Story released. This album references and is named after the movie, "Friday Night Lights".

On May 4, 2007, J. Cole released his debut mixtape, The Come Up. He produced 14 out of the 21 tracks on the mixtape himself, including his debut single, “Simba,” which he shot his first music video for.

Born Sinner is J. Cole’s sophomore studio album and the follow-up to his 2011 album Cole World: The Sideline Story.The album’s title is inspired by a line from The Notorious B.I.G.’s 1994 track “Juicy,” which is sampled on the album’s opener, “Villuminati”.

As a thoughtful, contemplative rapper, J. Cole generally stays away from beefing with other artists or living a gaudy, front-page lifestyle. He runs the Dreamville Records label with his former St. John's University classmate Ibrahim Hamad, releasing music by up-and-coming artists including Omen, Bas and Cozz.

The label shares a name with his Dreamville Foundation, a non-profit Cole established in his hometown. Set up to “bridge the gap between the worlds of opportunity and the urban youth of Fayetteville, NC,” it runs a series of events and programs, including a reading club and essay contests.

Cole is married to Melissa Heholt, with whom he has a child. He recently purchased the childhood home after which he named his 2014 Forest Hills Drive album, with the intention of developing it into temporary rent-free accommodation for single mothers.

The Warm Up is J Cole’s second mixtape and the leading factor in him being signed to Roc Nation, which significantly is home to “Lights Please,” the song that convinced Jay-Z to sign him. It is also the first basketball themed project in his early discography.

On March 31st, 2015, it became the first hip-hop album in 25 years to be certified platinum without any features.

The title track of 4 Your Eyez Only serves as a message to both J. Cole's newborn little girl and Nina the daughter of a deceased childhood friend, whom the rapper calls James McMillian, Jr.

KOD is J. Cole’s long-anticipated fifth studio album. The 12-track album serves as a follow up to his 2016 album, 4 Your Eyez Only.
